## Four-axis scorecard

**Axis 1 — Protocol validity.** The plan scopes the card as a discovery-surface
landing page for the bundle artifact, not as a methods page. That is the
correct frame for HF: the methodology authority remains `cogstructurelab.com`
and `ARCHITECTURE.md §1.5`, with the card linking out. The three-step CDA
elicitation (free-list / pile-sort / pile-interview) is implicit in the bundle
content and is named in the bundle README — repeating it briefly is fine,
re-deriving it is out of scope. No protocol concerns.

**Axis 2 — Analytical validity.** Surfacing five raw counts
(informants / failures / decline_interviews / models / domains) is descriptive,
not inferential — no register-1/2 statistics, no CIs, no rank claims. These
counts are bundle-shape facts, not measurement claims. Acceptable. The card
must not surface OCI, Smith's S, CCM, Procrustes, or any inferential number;
those belong on the dashboard with their uncertainty surface. The plan does
not propose them, which is correct.

**Axis 3 — Claims validity.** The framing constraints (§1.5 corpus lens,
§1.5.4 forbidden vocab, §1.5.5 no-human-baseline) are correctly carried
forward. Two carry-risks for the draft: (a) the §6.6 stale text at L1580
("The HuggingFace dataset card repeats the CC-BY-4.0 and Romney attribution
requirement") pre-dates the §1.5.5 amendment and must not be propagated
verbatim into the card; (b) the "How to use" section invites a measurement
reading if not voiced as "we ran the protocol; here is what came out" per
§1.5.7. See N1, N3, N4 below.

**Axis 4 — Audience translation.** HF discovery surface skews ML practitioner.
The dominant misread to defend against is "LSB is a benchmark for model
selection / leaderboard." §1.5.6 + §1.5.7 are the antidote, but they need an
explicit, near-the-top sentence in this card — readers who never click through
to `ARCHITECTURE.md` must still land on the exploratory frame. See N2.

## Per-decision rulings

**1. CC0 vs CC-BY-4.0 in YAML.** `license: cc0-1.0` is **correct**. The bundle
artifact (the tarball this card describes) is CC0 per §6.6 row 4 and
`LICENSE-OPENBUNDLE`. The M7 prep doc's `cc-by-4.0` was wrong. The §6.6 prose
at L1580 referencing "CC-BY-4.0 and Romney attribution" on the HF card is
**stale text** superseded by the 2026-05-07 §1.5.5 amendment and by the
SME-approved bundle README, which is CC0 and treats Romney as forebear, not
as bundled data. The card YAML follows the bundle README, not the stale §6.6
line. (T14 follow-up: §6.6 L1580 needs a doc-fix to align with the amendment;
non-blocking for T7.1.)

**2. Romney 1996 attribution in the card.** **Omit as legal-attribution
requirement; include as ancestry credit only.** Romney data is not in the
bundle (per §1.5.5 and bundle README L24–26), so there is no CC-BY object on
HF to attribute. The card should mirror the bundle README's exact treatment:
Romney named alongside D'Andrade / Weller / Borgatti / Batchelder as
methodological forebears, in a single sentence, with a pointer to the
methodology page for fuller citation. No "additional attribution requirement"
language. See N3.

**3. `task_categories: [other]`.** **Accept.** HF's task taxonomy is built
around supervised-learning task families (classification, generation,
question-answering, etc.). CDA elicitation is none of those; it is structured
generation analyzed off-platform. Forcing `text-generation` would mis-signal
the artifact as a training corpus. `[other]` is the methodologically honest
hint. The body text is where the actual nature of the artifact is conveyed.

**4. Statistics in the card.** **Acceptable with framing constraint.** Raw
counts of bundle objects (informants/failures/decline_interviews/models/
domains) are dataset-shape facts, in the same class as "this CSV has N rows."
They do not imply a measurement claim. They must be voiced as bundle
inventory ("this bundle contains N informant records across M models and D
domains"), not as findings ("LSB measured N..."). The "Dataset statistics"
header itself is fine; the prose inside must not drift toward leaderboard
voice. See N4.

**5. Forward-looking misread risk.** **The plan does not adequately hedge
against the "LSB is a model-selection benchmark" misread.** This is the
single largest framing risk for the HF surface and the reason this verdict
is PASS-WITH-NOTES rather than PASS. ML researchers landing from
`huggingface.co/datasets` will pattern-match "benchmark" to leaderboard
artifacts they have seen before. The card needs an explicit, early sentence
that names what LSB is *not* (a capability benchmark, a leaderboard, a
model-quality ranking) alongside what it is (a reproducible elicitation
protocol whose outputs are released for community interpretation). See N2.

## Binding notes for the Coder (apply when drafting)

**N1 — License surface.** YAML uses `license: cc0-1.0`. The card body's
License section says CC0 1.0 Universal applies to the bundle artifact this
card describes, with one sentence noting that the in-repo working data
(`data/raw/`, `data/processed/`) carries CC-BY-4.0 per §6.6 and is a separate
distribution. Do **not** propagate the stale §6.6 L1580 phrasing about a
CC-BY-4.0-plus-Romney requirement on the HF card itself.

**N2 — Anti-leaderboard sentence, early.** Within the first two paragraphs
of the body, include a sentence (or short paragraph) that names what LSB is
not: not a capability benchmark, not a leaderboard, not a model-quality
ranking. Pair it with the §1.5.7 "we ran the protocol; here is what came
out" frame. The bundle README's opening already does this implicitly via
"draw your own conclusions" + "the mismatch is the finding"; the HF card
must do it explicitly because the HF audience pattern-matches "benchmark"
to leaderboard.

**N3 — Romney treatment.** Mirror the bundle README's exact treatment of
Romney (L24–26): named alongside D'Andrade, Weller, Borgatti, Batchelder as
methodological forebears in a single sentence; explicitly state the data is
not in this bundle; point to the methodology page on `cogstructurelab.com`
for fuller citation. No standalone "Romney attribution requirement" header.
No CC-BY-with-attribution language on the card.

**N4 — Statistics voiced as inventory.** The "Dataset statistics" section
prose voices counts as bundle inventory ("This bundle contains N informant
records produced by M models across D domains; K sessions were preserved as
failures and L as decline interviews"), not as measurement findings. No
phrasing like "LSB measured N..." or "LSB found that...". No comparative
language ("more than", "the largest"). Raw counts, neutral voicing.

**N5 — Corpus lens framing line.** Within the "What this dataset is"
section, the corpus lens construct is named once in long form per §1.5.1
("the latent categorical structure of a training corpus, refracted through
training and alignment") with the short form ("corpus lens") used
thereafter. The bundle README L21–23 is the template; reuse the voice.

**N6 — Forbidden vocabulary scan.** The §1.5.4 table is binding on the card
text. The drafted card will get a forbidden-vocab spot-check in the second
SME pass: `worldview`, `believes`, `thinks`, "within-model consensus",
"within-model eigenratio", "cultural bias" (standalone), "publishable",
"closer to human", "LSB hypothesizes/tested/confirms/predicts". Zero
tolerance.

**N7 — Methodology pointer, not methodology re-derivation.** The card links
to `ARCHITECTURE.md §1.5` and `cogstructurelab.com` methodology page but
does not re-derive methodology. One paragraph of context, one paragraph of
framing (per N2 and N5), then files / how-to-use / license / citation /
links. Resist the urge to inline the five-link chain (§1.5.1) or the
limitations list (§1.5.3); link to them.

**N8 — Tag list discipline.** `tags` from the T11 GitHub topics list is
acceptable as long as no tag is itself forbidden vocabulary (e.g., a tag
literally reading `worldview` or `cultural-bias` would fail). The drafter
should review the T11 list with §1.5.4 in hand before copying. If T11
includes any taxonomy-adjacent phrasing that conflicts, omit those tags
from the HF card and route the conflict back to the Architect.
